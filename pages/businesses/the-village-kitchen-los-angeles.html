      Review from Ann E.

      •  
      • 12 friends
      • 40 reviews
      • Ann E.

      Sherman Oaks, CA

      8/24/2011

      You know, I was impressed (and if you look at my reviews, it's not easy to do). The food's pretty good, the prices are more than fair and they make red velvet cupcakes to boot. You'll get a better deal here than at 99% of the food trucks roaming LA. That's a promise (no, for legal reasons, not really).

      I ordered the Colossus burger (a regular burger with Swiss and pastrami) for $5.50 and a red velvet. For $5.50, the burger was huge; I took a third of it to go. The patty was moist, the bun was good. The red velvet was subtle, moist and not cloying. They didn't feel the need to clobber you with sugar to let you know it was a red velvet. It was refreshing.

      The bottom line is: there's no reason to not be a regular patron of The Village Kitchen. In case you're worried that you might eat all the cupcakes, let me assure you, yes, it could happen. But any guilt you may feel from eating the burger and cupcakes will immediately be mollified by the wonderful (and unwarranted) feeling of generosity. The proceeds go to the women and children's center. Really, if you don't eat here, it's safe to say that you're simply a bad person.

      This lovely little place is only a few blocks from where I work, and I don't know why it's taken me so long to check it out, but I did today and I was amply rewarded.  I had the chicken sandwich, and I am here to state that it was just about the best sandwich I have ever, ever had.  And I've had my share.  The chicken was moist and fresh and tasted as though it had just a hint of citrus to it.  It was served with perfectly roasted red peppers and grilled onions on a to-die-for freshly baked baguette.

    About This Business

    Provided by business

    Specialties

    We are a scratch bakery cafe specializing in cakes, cookies, breakfast baked treats and award winning cupcakes.  We also feature a seasonal lunch menu comprised of fresh salads (cobb, thai beef, nicoise, caesar )just to name a few, and hand carved sandwiches (grilled chicken, roasted turkey, roasted ham, hot pastrami and more) Call us and hear about our Daily specials-grilled salmon BLT, seared tuna sandwich, hamburger tuesdays, and our mouth watering bi-weekly BBQ.

    History

    Established in 2008

    The Village Kitchen is a vocational training center whose mission is to provide on the job training in the culinary arts to recently homeless or otherwise employment challenged women.  We are a program of The Good Shepherd Center for Homeless Women and Children.  Our program is innovative and much needed.  Our hope is to develop experienced cooks, bakers, and waiters over the course of 6-9 months, so that they can go out and compete for more profitable employment, breaking historic cycles of poverty.
