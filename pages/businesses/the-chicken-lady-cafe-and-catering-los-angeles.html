      I have a friend who is obsessed with this place. And since she is from Alabama I take her recommendation very seriously.  So I was super excited to find myself free for lunch with enough time to head over to the Chicken Lady for some tasty fare.

      I appreciate the effort that is put forth in terms of ambiance--the cafe is in a dull strip mall on the corner of a super busy intersection. So there are cute tables and tons of fake flowers/greenery to block out the streets/ cars. Nice touch. The food is all quite affordable--around $9 for TONS of food. Which would be great if I actually wanted that much of any of it, which sadly I do not.

      The chicken tenders were above average, nice and juicy with a medium fry on the outside. Most important of all, the BBQ sauce was the right one (of the Sweet Baby Rays variety) versus some of the sad sack sauces I have seen at other places. The "Thai" tenders tasted like someone had dumped the normal tenders into Kraft sweet n sour sauce for about an hour, and the "Mesquite cilantro and lime" tasted nothing like Mesquite, Cilantro or Lime. Mostly just some honey-ish type cloying flavor.

      The cornbread was decent (if dry) and the salad was nice (if a bit small). We both went with the mac n cheese for a side, which was quite tasty but clearly the fattiest thing I have ever eaten in LA. I clearly could only finish about half, and yet my stomach still hurt for about an hour afterwards because of all the fat and sugar.

      So I guess if you can handle that kind of thing (but which I mean eating like a drunk frat boy) go for it...

      Long Beach, CA

      9/24/2011

      I've been wanting to try this little (and by little I really mean tiny) place for ages. I saw it reviewed on the news several years ago and it really piqued my interest. They were are ranked in the top 5 lunch spots and top 10 American food restaurants in Los Angeles for 2009. The chicken lady's primary venture is catering to the movie and television industry, but lucky for us we have the opportunity to try her great food as well.  She's located on San Vicente just of La Cienega in a small strip mall with very little parking, however there is a valet on site. As I mentioned the place is small, I believe it only seats 16 inside and has 2 tables for 2 outside - it's all very tight - but they are so friendly they make you feel like you're eating in mom's kitchen.

      The claim to fame here are the chicken tenders prepared several different ways. So my friends and I found Doris Day parking and were able to sit right down and order.  My friend's all ordered the traditional chicken tenders, which is served with a small salad of mixed greens and a wedge of corn bread, as well as your choice of one side - the Mac N' Cheese seems to be te popular side and this is what my friends ordered. I ventured away from the traditional and ordered a mixed plate of the mesquite lime cilantro tenders as well as a Thai stick with the Lady's sexy sauce.  Everyone raved about their food. And the plates are only $8.99 - a great value!

      When we were finishing our meal we had got to meet her - that's right! - the Chicken Lady herself came out to greet us and apologize for the noise back in the kitchen because they were hustling to get a big order together for the cast and staff of "Two and a Half Men."  If that wasn't nice enough she also provided us dessert with her compliments - a small wedge of french cheese pie accompanied with the cutiest little scoop of vanilla ice cream I've ever seen.  Needless to say we were completely won over by this - not that they didn't have us at hello!  Terrific group of people.

      Be prepared - you are not going to be served on fine china here - they are a catering business and you are going to get served on paper and plastic. Personally I felt that added to the fun, felt like we were being catered to just like the big studios.  Also she serves a variety of specials - Thursdays and Fridays - Chicken Pot Pie, Grilled Salmon, Baby Back Ribs, Roasted Chicken, and more...She's only open for lunch from 11am-2:30pm, M-F.  Treat yourself and do something different for lunch next time you're shopping at the BC and go across the street and around the corner to this little gem!
