      Review from Mandy d.

      • Elite '11

      • Mandy d.

      Los Angeles, CA

      7/5/2011 1 Check-in Here

      Ethiopian Food AND Comfort Food? I'll take it!

      I had purchased a gift certificate for Industry Cafe and Jazz from http://restaurant.com for $25 off when you spent $35 or more, and my boyfriend, being the sweetheart that he is, said he would try Ethiopian food with me, since I really wanted to try more since my experience at Nyala.

      Industry is located in a part of Culver City that I hadn't seen before, behind the big Helms Building. The restaurant itself isn't very large, but its not too tiny. We went around 7:30 on a Saturday and were seated immediately, it was about half full. The interior has big drapes that swish over the ceiling and cool abstract paintings on the walls.

      We ordered some wine (they only have honey wine from Ethiopia all the rest were from California, which was kind of a shame considering how much I loved the wine I had before) at about $8 a glass, but the glass was full! Since we had to rack up $35 in food charges, we got the fried chicken with macaroni & cheese, the fried chicken was tasty, but I was really impressed with the macaroni and cheese, it was very very cheesy.  We also got two Ethiopian beef dishes (I can't remember the names).  One was chunks of beef with peppers and onions, it was good, but the meat was a little tough.  The other one was amazing.  You can get the beef either medium rare, or rare and it actually is ground up with spices.  Its spicy but so flavorful and delicious.  It came with sides of veggies so we got the collard greens and yellow lentils that were delicious when eaten with the injara.  They give you a good amount of food (dishes cost about $11-$13) and we had enough leftovers for at least another meal or two.

      They had a live jazz band that played throughout the night that was really good.  They were loud, but they would only play about 15-20 minute sets before taking a break so that you could hear your company again.   There was only one waitress that I saw throughout the night, she was really nice, but be prepared to spend a few hours here (we spend about 21/2-3 ) since the food takes a little while to cook and the atmosphere is laid back and lingering.

      I had a really great time here, and its a wonderful place to go on a date.  You eat with your hands and share a plate, with dim lighting and jazz music playing, a great place to woo that special someone.

      Listed in: Westsiiiiiiidddeeee, Love is in the Air

      We went to dinner at Industry Cafe and Jazz this weekend, mostly because it was an option on http://Restaurant.com, but also because we had driven by a few times and it looked like a pretty cool place to try out. Plus, I'm kind of a sucker for live music.

      4.5 stars for the music. The live jazz was everything I'd hoped for. Mellow but fun, pleasant, and unpretentious. Perfect for a relaxing Friday night dinner.

      3 stars for the food. At first I liked the idea that the menu featured Ethiopian meals right alongside soulfood, but when the food came out  and I dug into my beef tibese, the smell of my boyfriend's fried catfish and mac and cheese wafting over was somewhat unappealing. It was kind of like eating cereal next to someone dining on spaghettios. The tibese was good, but the fried catfish and soulfood sides left something to be desired. I've certainly had better.

      If I were on a time schedule, I would have given one star for the service, because the food took FOREVER to come out (we waited more than 10 minutes just to get the bottled beers we ordered), the waitress neglected to bring us the waters we asked for, and she never came by to check if things were okay or if we wanted a second round of drinks. It took at least 20 minutes after we had finished eating to get the check. Because we weren't in any sort of hurry, though, the waitress's neglect was kind of nice. There was no hovering, no rushing us. We could have sat there forever. And we did.

      The owner came by our table at the end and chatted with us for a bit, which based on these other yelp reviews, I guess he does often. He was very friendly and likable, and offered us a free dessert (ice cream with yams) since it was our first time in the restaurant. Yum!

      Averaging all the above, I think this place is decidedly three stars. A-Ok, but with room to improve.

      Also, a note: drinks aren't included with the http://restaurant.com certificates, which makes meeting the $35 minimum a little difficult if there are just two people dining. I recommend using it with a bigger group. No complaint there, though, since that just means their prices are good!!

      Disclaimer: I do not know much about Ethiopian food, so proceed with caution when reading my review.

      I bought a DealPop voucher for this place and rushed to use it before it expired.  Gf and I came here for lunch on Sunday, roughly at 12:30 pm.  We parked on the street right in front of the restaurant and before we even entered, I had an inkling we would be the only patrons inside.  Sure enough, the place was empty...not even a server.  I walked toward the back and hollered to see if anyone was inside.  A sweet Ethiopian woman, who was the cook, came out and greeted us.  However, she could not speak English so she whipped out her cell phone, called some random guy and handed me the phone.  On the other end was someone who I assumed to be the owner, as he was explaining how the DealPop voucher works.  I placed our order with Mr. Phone Server and handed the phone back to the cook.  I wondered if this would be the future of the restaurant business: no servers, just cooks, use phones/computers to place orders.

      Yelp, I hate it when you totally let me down!!

      I was heading to Industry to watch a friend play some jazz on a Tuesday night.  I was absolutely STARVING and could have eaten dinner at home, but I checked out the Yelp reviews for this place and saw that most people said the food was great.  I decided to wait it out and spend my precious recession dollars on a meal out.

      The venue itself is cool - small, laid back decor, a little noisy because of the jazz but whatever, I think most (if not all) people there were friends of the band anyway.  I was so excited to have found a hole in the wall place with WHAT I THOUGHT would be great food.

      After waiting what seemed like an eternity for the waitress to come back to our table to take our order, I ordered the Lamb Tibisi and my two companions both ordered fried chicken with various sides.  I don't consider myself an expert on Ethiopian cuisine by any means, but I've dined at 4 (I think) Ethiopian restaurants in 3 different cities so I had a general idea of what to expect.

      After another looooong wait, the food was delivered.  I enthusiastically grabbed a piece of injera (the spongy bread) and dug in.  On my second or third bite, I shoveled what looked like a chunk of lamb into my mouth, only to find out that it was a LARGE CHUNK OF SHARP BONE.  I'm not sure if this is traditional, but there was about a 1:1 ratio of meat to bone shards in my dish.  It made the rest of the experience less than enjoyable as I had to poke and smoosh every bite before eating to make sure I wasn't going to choke on a bone chunk and spend my last moments on earth listening to jazz, which I don't even really like.  I found several small and sharp chunks of bone hidden in my dish as well, which just didn't seem right.

      Even despite the various dangers associated with my dinner, the food was really just so-so.  The lamb was tough and chewy, and not that flavorful.  I asked for my dish with medium spice and it was totally mild, bordering on bland.  The side of soggy veggies was similarly uninspired, and it came with a little salad which I thought was lame considering I wanted Ethiopian food, not a sad side salad that looked like it belonged at a pizza joint.

      My fried chicken friends were similarly unimpressed.  The chicken was dry and generally unappetizing.  I tried a bite of the candied yams which were decent, but a dish can not survive on sides alone.

      The service was pretty much non-existent - we never got a check even when we were long done eating, so each member of our party had to go up to the bar and wait for quite a while to pay.

      Overall, Industry was pretty lame.  I'd go for the music and a drink because it's pretty laid back, but stay away from the food!  It wasn't even cheap enough to somewhat excuse its mediocrity.
