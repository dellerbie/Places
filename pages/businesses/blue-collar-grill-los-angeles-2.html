      Woodland Hills, CA

      7/25/2011

      I was really looking forward to finding a decent Italian beef sandwich in LA, especially in the Valley, so I wouldn't have to drive to Portillo's in OC or fly to Chicago. So when I found BCG on Yelp, and read the fantastic reviews, I was very excited!

      Unfortunately, the Italian beef is just not right.

      They use the wrong cut of beef, they slice it wrong, they season it wrong (it barely tasted like a hint of sweet basil and oregano.... BARELY), there were no green peppers, the giardiniera was really just some small pickled jalapeños. Also, it took WAAAAAAAYY too long to get the order, and it was well after the lunch rush.  Seriously, a IB sand shouldn't take more than a few minutes to make, I waited almost 20 minutes for it. VERY DISAPPOINTING to say the least. ONE STAR.

      What was good about BCG is the bread used on the sandwich, not quite an Italian sandwich roll, but A very close French roll replica. Tasty still. Also, the people there are very friendly, good crowd of customers and the employees made it enjoyable. TWO STARS.

      If you are a stoner in the Valley looking for some seriously fatty, greasy, cheesy, deep fried grub, this is your spot.

      If you want a real authentic Italian Beef in So. Cal., this is not the place. Drive to Portillo's in OC and get a beef / Maxwell Polish combo with peppers.

      This is my first time writing a review about anything...but I feel particularly called to write about this place, 1. I'm from Philly and I am always craving the perfect steak sandwich and 2. I'm picky, and 3. I know yelp helps people like me navigate where to go - so I want to give back.

      Blue Collar Grill is an unassuming hole-in-the-wall within an unimpressive strip in Canoga Park. I just happened to walk by and decided to give it a try. The decorum was intriguing if not rough - urban red walls with writings of rave reviews all over - so I thought this could be promising. There are a few small tables - very cramped. I took my sandwich to go.

      The wait was longer than expected - probably 10 minutes, which is a lot considering I get 60 for lunch. The price was fair - $7 for the steak sandwich, which includes cheese, sautéed onions, peppers and mushrooms. Good portion!

      When I got to the office - a short walking distance away - I discovered my sandwich was drenched in grease inside the paper-lined box! The roll, which was not the advertised Amarosa roll, as they ran out - was soggy! Outside of that rather distracting messiness, it was tasty. It tasted pretty darned authentic!

      Next time, I will order ahead and ask that they drain the sandwich before placing it into the box. It always helps to be proactive. Also, I discovered, come in before Wednesday, as Wednesday nights he gets swamped and runs out of rolls!

      I hope that helps!

      Review from Andrea C.

      •  
      • 18 friends

      • Andrea C.

      La Crescenta-Montrose, CA

      7/13/2011

      First of all let me say that I am a die-hard philly cheesesteak fan. I have had them in Philly and in Chicago and I'm a pretty tough judge of what's good. Just meat and cheese on a roll does not a philly make.

      That being said, I LOVE Blue Collar Grill. The guys running it are the nicest guys you will ever meet, their food is spot-on and delivered to your table (or office) with a smile. They seem genuinely pleased that you chose to have lunch with them.

      As for the food, I had the philly and the fresh potato chips. The sandwich was a large portion, but I had to eat every bite. The white american cheese was like molten lava, permeating the sandwich with its gooey goodness. The beef was nicely cooked and chopped (not just sliced) and the sandwich includes mushrooms, onions and peppers (as it should.) The fresh chips were hot and crispy and large enough for the table to share.

      Earlier reviews said that they only have canned soda, but there is a soda fountain if you dine in, as well as sweet and unsweet tea.

      The prices include tax, which makes ordering and paying easy. Sandwiches are $5.50-7, which is more than reasonable for what you get.

      If I could give them more stars, I would. I'll be back to try their beef sandwich but I will have a hard time not getting the philly every time. The fact that I work nearby means I'm going to have to up the workouts...but it's worth it.

      Review from Bill C.

      •  
      • 147 friends
      • 454 reviews
      • Bill C.

      Encino, CA

      8/14/2010 4 photos

      I saw the ad in LA Weekly, free chips and a can of soda with the purchase of a Philly Cheesesteak.  And any business that manages to use the words "free" and "cheesesteak" in the same sentence is going to see me walking through the door.

      The cheesesteak is only $6.00 (including tax) and is a good deal at that price.  Mushrooms, onions and red bell peppers are already included in that price, so there are no add-ons.  They are made fresh to order.  It was served on a bakery bun.  Not the Amarosa roll one would expect, but the next best thing.

      Now if I had read the ad carefully, I would have seen that the free soda and chips special was only from 3 to 5 on weekdays, but the nice lady split the difference with me.   The Coke Zero and the chips were a dollar each, so even though it was lunchtime on a Saturday, she gave me one of them for free.

      The chips were homemade, and were served in a big basket.  everyone of them was crispy and warm.

      One drawback is they don't have a soda fountain.  It's canned sodas only.  Hopefully they will add a fountain soon.

      True to its name the Blue Collar Grill isn't a place you want to take your vegan friends.  This is a place for those of our species who are proud to be the top of the food chain.

      Next time, I want to try the Italian beef sandwich or maybe the chili size. They also have several hot dogs on the menu, apparently all from Vienna beef.  While they have some New York photos on the walls, most are from Chicago, Philadelphia or the owners original hometown Pittsburgh----three cities where it's easy to find good working guy American food.

      If it's burgers, Philly Cheese-steak and gourmet dogs that you want or need, this is the place. And it's not just good food. It's great! And at a really good price for what you get. I have had two Philly cheese-steak sandwiches, Two burgers, and the Italian Beef Dip sandwich. All were fantastic. I couldn't be happier to have this place open up in my town.
      The location certainly made me raise an eyebrow when I saw the sign go up. I would not have been surprised to see the 10,000th Taquería or another Indian place. There is plenty of parking for now, unless they get really popular.
      Walking through the door is like walking through a portal from Mexifornia to maybe Chicago. Not that I don't love my multi-cultural 'hood, it's just nice to get away now and then without taking a jet. I think it is supposed to be a Chicago theme and it certainly does make you feel like you are not in Canoga Park anymore. I thank them for that.  
      I'm a big fan and I wish them success. The owners are doing the work and are super nice and attentive. They obviously care about their labor of love and care about providing a great meal.
